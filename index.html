Myanmar currently faces significant internet infrastructure challenges, including inconsistent connection speeds, frequent service interruptions, and network throttling in some regions. These issues can cause Juche TV videos to buffer heavily, fail to load, or disconnect mid-stream.

Tips for Myanmar users:
• Lower the quality — go to Settings and select 480p or lower to reduce buffering on slow connections.
• Use Wi-Fi when available — mobile data in many areas is too slow for live streams; fixed broadband or fibre works better.
• Try YouTube-sourced videos first — they are hosted on Google's servers which tend to have better local caching in Myanmar than our direct MP4 streams.
• During outages — if your internet is cut off entirely, unfortunately no streaming is possible. The Juche TV app shell will still load from cache if you have previously installed it as a PWA.
• If power outages occur frequently in your area — we strongly recommend taking advantage of any moment when your internet is available to download videos and movies from Juche TV in advance. Having content saved locally means you can continue enjoying your favourite films and performances offline, completely uninterrupted — even during extended power cuts or network blackouts.

We are aware of connectivity issues in Myanmar and are exploring lower-bitrate stream options for future updates.
